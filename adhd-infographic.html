Understanding ADHD

A Parent's Guide to Thriving

What is ADHD, Really?

It’s not a behavior problem or a parenting fail. ADHD is a common neurodevelopmental condition. Think of it as a brain with a high-performance engine but a different kind of braking and steering system. This can make it hard to regulate attention, impulses, and emotions.

~8.4%
of children are estimated to have ADHD.

Your child isn't "lazy" or "naughty"—their brain just has a unique operating system!

The Three Faces of ADHD

🧠

Inattentive

  • Gets sidetracked easily
  • Trouble following instructions
  • Often loses things
  • Seems to not be listening
  • Makes careless mistakes
⚡️

Hyperactive-Impulsive

  • Constantly fidgeting/squirming
  • Difficulty staying seated
  • Talks excessively & interrupts
  • Blurts out answers
  • Trouble waiting their turn
🧠⚡️

Combined

The most common presentation, where a child shows a significant number of symptoms from both lists.

How Common is Each Type?

The "Combined Type" is the most frequently diagnosed presentation of ADHD.

Building a Support System

👨‍🏫

Behavioral Therapy

Learn strategies to help your child build skills, create routines, and manage big emotions.

🏫

School Accommodations

An IEP or 504 Plan can provide support like extra time on tests or help with organization.

💊

Medication

A safe and effective option for many to help balance brain chemistry. Discuss with your doctor.

🏃‍♂️

Lifestyle Support

Consistent routines, healthy diet, regular exercise, and sufficient sleep make a huge difference.
